 **Preface**
 
 **10/05/2026**

&emsp;This is a journal I have written regarding prime integers, that is, positive integers, above 1, that only have two divisors: itself and 1. Because of the chaotic nature of prime integers, it is difficult to determine a formula to find the $n^{\text{th}}$ prime number in an infinite sequence of prime numbers. Thus far, the very few formulae that have been envisioned up until this point are inefficient and slow, mainly the [Willans’ Formula of 1964](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/WillansFormula.html).

&emsp;This is in no way a professional mathematics journal. I have no faith that I’ll be able to find the definitive mathematical formula in determining the $n^{\text{th}}$ prime integer. (May 10, 2026)
